Disproportionality in Special Education

The purpose of this project was to look for evidence of disproportionality in variables that may influence special education enrollment in the state of Minnesota. Disproportionality is the over or under-representation of a particular demographic relative to the presence of the group in the overall student population. Disproportionality can have adverse affects on student achievement, fuel negative stereotypes, and prevent schools from meeting the needs of a diverse student body.

For this project, the factors studied were community poverty rates, population density, rates of students of color, and rates of English language learners. Using data from the Minnesota Department of Education and the Census Bureau, we used Matplotlib to run linear regressions to look for a correlation. Although several national studies have proven that disproportionality exists, this project was unable to show correlation in our data sets. Performing similar analysis on other data sources might produce better results.

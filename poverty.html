Do districts with higher poverty rates have higher rates of identified special education students?

R-squared = 0.16198
Correlation = 0.4

The percent of the population in a district living under the federal poverty level (according to the Census Bureau) is along the x-axis, and the y-axis represents the percent of students receiving special education services. The correlation coefficient of 0.4 suggests that there might be a correlation between poverty in a district and the percent of students receiving special ed services that should be examined more closely. One variation would be to look at the percentage of families with school-aged children living in poverty, as opposed to the population as a whole.

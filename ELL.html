Do districts with a higher rate of English Language Learners have a higher rate of identified special education students?

R-squared = 0.00538
Correlation = -0.07

The x-axis represents the percent of English Language Learners in a district, with the y-axis representing the percent of students receiving special education services. Visually, it appears that there are more districts with a smaller percentage of English language learners. A correlation coefficient of -0.07 indicates that there is not much association between these two data sets. It would be interesting to look at how each district's percentage of special education students compares to the percentage of English Language Learners in the district's overall population.
